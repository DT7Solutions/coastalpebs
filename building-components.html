Turbo Ventilators operate by using the energy of the wind to induce airflow by the centrifugal  action. As the name suggests, the turbo ventilators are the ventilators powered by the wind to create effective ventilation for various industries. They are round metal vents with fins in them. The faster the wind, the faster is the rotation of the turbine. The force caused by spinning vents creates a region of low pressure area which draws out air through the turbine. The air drawn is continuously replaced by the fresh air from surroundings. Even the lower velocity energy of the wind will cause the turbine to rotate. Suction is maintained even at the lower wind velocities.

A louver is a ventilation product that allows air to pass through it, keeping out the unwanted dirt, debris and water. The blades overlap each other and assist in free air flow. A water resistant louver is characterized by an aesthetically pleasing two piece front louver frame affixed to a posteriorly positioned aluminum mesh screen in turn affixed to a spacer frame behind it that encompasses a plurality of horizontally parallel deflector slats affixed to a face plate affixed to the frontal portion of a rear louver frame attached to the screen and front frame via a plurality of fastening screws with all frames and the screen affixed to a positively sloped bottom sill plate, the top side of which is metallically lined to accept water deflected from the slots and a drip cap.

Trims and Flashings

  • Trim generally refers to a material used to finish and cover a joint or junction to improve the outer look.
  • Flashing is used to describe a material for joining two components together to provide proper weather tightness.
  • The trims and flashings make the building look really good. Flashing is important for maintaining the building’s protection. Properly matched colors and installed trim can turn a rough looking building into an aesthetically appealing marvel. Trim is specially made by press brake so it can be designed specifically for your needs. A basic sketch with length and gauge dimensions will assist us in helping to determine if the trim can be made.
